📖 How to Play
The Goal

You are a diver descending into a dungeon made entirely of playing cards. Your goal is simple: survive long enough to exhaust the entire 44-card deck. Clear every card without losing all your HP and you win.

💀 HP varies by character. If your HP reaches zero at any point during a room — even mid-resolution — the run ends immediately. A potion later in the same selection cannot save you.

Restore HP equal to their value, up to your maximum. Only 1 potion works per room — the second is wasted.

Equip to reduce monster damage. A weapon subtracts its value from incoming damage. Values 2–10 only.
Rooms — How Each Turn Works

Each room draws 4 cards from the deck. You must select 3 of them to resolve — the 4th is automatically carried over into the next room as its first card.

🃏 Cards resolve in the order you select them. Order matters — equipping a weapon before fighting a monster is very different from fighting first.

When the deck runs out and you've resolved all remaining cards, you win.

Weapons — The Kill Chain

Equipping a weapon lets you reduce damage from monsters — but there's a catch. Your weapon only works against monsters strictly weaker than the last one you defeated with it. If the next monster is equal to or stronger than your last kill, the weapon won't help.

⚠️ Example: You equip a 7♦ and defeat a 5♠ (took 0 damage). Next you face a 6♣ — your weapon is ineffective because 6 ≥ 5. You take full 6 damage.

The kill chain resets every time you equip a new weapon. Plan your order: fight the strongest monster first, then work downward.

🖱️ Right-click a monster (or long-press on mobile) to bypass your weapon — you take full damage but your kill chain is preserved for the other monsters in the room.
Skip — Escaping a Room

Once per room (with a one-room cooldown), you can press Skip to send all room cards to the bottom of the deck and draw a fresh room. Skip is free — no HP cost — but it's disabled on the final room of the run.

🏃 Use Skip when the room has too many high-value monsters your weapon can't handle. Since skipped cards go to the bottom of the deck, you'll see them again eventually — plan accordingly.
Characters

Every character plays differently. Deckard Diver is always available — straightforward rules, 20 HP, no gimmicks. Additional characters can be unlocked by achieving specific feats during your runs. Each unlocked character brings a unique passive ability that changes how the deck, weapons, or rooms behave.

🔒 Unlock conditions are hidden until met. Try different strategies and see what opens up.
Profiles

Deck Diver supports up to 3 separate profiles, each with their own unlocks, stats, and save data. Select your profile from the profile screen before entering the main menu. You can create, rename, or delete profiles at any time, and switch between them using the Switch Profile option on the main menu.

Strategy Tips
🔢
Kill High to Low
Defeat the biggest monster first so your weapon stays effective against the weaker ones that follow.
🗡️
Keep Your Weapon
Don't swap weapons unless necessary. Swapping resets your kill chain, and the new weapon's chain carries into the next room.
🧪
Time Your Potions
Only one potion works per room. If you draw two, carry the weaker one over and drink the higher-value one.
🃏
Choose Your Skip
A room with two high monsters and no weapon is dangerous — Skip out. But remember those cards go back into the deck.
🖱️
Bypass Wisely
Right-clicking forces full damage but saves your kill chain. Use it when a monster would break your chain anyway.
❤️
Watch the Preview
The health bar shows a live damage/healing preview as you select cards. Use it before confirming every room.
💀
Order Kills You
If your selections would kill you mid-room, the run ends there — a potion selected after a lethal hit won't save you.
🔄
Carry-Over is a Resource
The card you leave behind carries into the next room. Intentionally leaving a potion or weapon can set up a powerful next room.
∞ Endless Mode

Endless Mode is a continuous gauntlet where the dungeon never truly ends. Instead of one run, you descend level after level — each level is a full deck run, and clearing it advances you to the next.

💜 Your HP carries over between levels. You don't get a full restore — every point of HP you conserve matters across the entire run.

Scaling difficulty: Each level adds one additional Tag — a modifier that changes the rules. Level 1 has no tags, Level 2 has 1, Level 3 has 2, and so on. Tags are randomly selected each level and can include effects like empowered monsters, disabled skipping, poisoned rooms, corrosive weapons, deceptive discard piles, and more.

After each level, you choose a Reward — upgrade a weapon, upgrade a potion, add a Curse Potion, gain a Crystal Ball (peek at upcoming cards), or gain a Warp Scroll (rearrange upcoming cards). Then a random unlocked character is offered as a swap — you can switch to them or keep your current character. Only one character is active at a time; abilities don't stack.

⚔ Boss Fights

Every 4th level (Level 4, 8, 12, etc.) is a Boss Fight. Bosses use a smaller, more dangerous deck and have a unique passive ability that changes the rules for that fight. The level's tags also apply on top of the boss's own ability.

⚠️ You enter the boss fight unarmed, with whatever HP you ended the previous level with. Dying in a boss fight ends your run immediately.

Boss levels carry the same tag scaling as any other level — a Level 8 boss will have 7 tags plus its own passive, making later bosses significantly more challenging.
